# My Reflection on Writing

## Writing Challenges and Difficulties

**What aspects of academic writing do you find most challenging?**
The most challenging part is balancing clarity and critical depth. I can summarize papers, but I often spend too much space on description and not enough on analytical comparison. Another challenge is writing precise claims without overgeneralization.

**What specific difficulties do you face when writing Introduction/Literature Review?**
For the Introduction, I find Move 2 difficult because identifying a niche requires both strong evidence and careful wording. For the Literature Review, I find synthesis difficult when papers use different assumptions and evaluation settings. It is easy to list findings paper by paper, but harder to build a coherent argument across them.

**What do you struggle with most?**
My biggest struggle is transforming reading notes into a clear research storyline. I need to improve how I connect background, gap, and contribution in a tighter logic flow.

## My Writing Process

**How do you approach writing Introduction and Literature Review?**
I usually start by selecting a focused topic and collecting 5-10 core papers. Then I extract key points into a comparison table, including problem setting, method, experiments, and limitations. After that, I draft the Introduction and Literature Review by moves, then revise for coherence.

**What steps do you take?**
1. Define topic scope and keywords.
2. Collect and screen representative papers.
3. Build a structured note table for each source.
4. Draft Introduction with three move structure.
5. Draft Literature Review with four move structure.
6. Revise for citation support, transitions, and concise language.

**Do you have a particular method or strategy?**
I use a gap first strategy during revision. I check whether each paragraph contributes to one of these goals: establishing territory, showing limitations in prior work, or motivating my research direction. If a paragraph does not serve one goal clearly, I rewrite or remove it.

## How I Use AI for Help

**Do you use AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) to help with writing?**
Yes. I mainly use ChatGPT and Cursor AI support.

**How do you use them?**
I use AI for brainstorming paragraph plans, refining sentence clarity, checking logical flow, and generating revision alternatives. I also use AI to convert rough notes into cleaner academic prose drafts that I manually edit.

**What prompts do you typically use?**
1. "Rewrite this paragraph for clearer academic logic while keeping my claims unchanged."
2. "Compare these two papers in terms of problem, method, and limitation in one paragraph."
3. "Check whether this introduction follows Move 1, Move 2, and Move 3."

**What do you find helpful or not helpful about AI assistance?**
AI is helpful for speed, structure, and language polishing. It is less reliable for nuanced claim strength and citation level precision, so I still need manual fact checking and source verification. I also need to avoid accepting fluent but unsupported statements.

## My Goals

**What do you hope to improve in your writing?**
I want to improve argument coherence, stronger gap articulation, and more critical synthesis across sources. I also want to make paragraph level transitions more natural.

**What specific skills do you want to develop?**
1. Writing sharper niche statements in Introduction Move 2.
2. Building synthesis paragraphs that compare multiple papers directly.
3. Improving citation grounded critical evaluation.
4. Editing for concise and precise academic language.
